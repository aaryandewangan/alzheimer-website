Alzheimer Disease

“Alzheimer's is not about the past—the successes, the accolades, the accomplishments, Its about he present and the struggle…”

Alzheimer's disease causes the brain to shrink and brain cells to eventually die. Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia — a gradual decline in memory, thinking, behavior and social skills. These changes affect a person's ability to function.

Memory loss and confusion are the main symptoms. No cure exists, but medication and management strategies may temporarily improve symptoms.
